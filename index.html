What is Full Self-Driving (Supervised)

Full Self-Driving (Supervised), also know as FSD, is Tesla’s advanced driver assistance system that enables vehicles to navigate roads with minimal human input. Using cameras, radar, and AI, it handles lane changes, traffic lights, stop signs, and complex maneuvers — all under driver supervision. Unlike traditional cruise control, FSD actively drives the car from point A to B, adapting in real time to traffic, pedestrians, and road conditions.

Proven safer than human drivers — with 85% fewer crashes per Tesla's latest data — FSD Supervised is ready for supervised use in Europe under Article 39 exemptions. It's not fully autonomous yet, but it's the closest step to safer, stress-free driving, already trusted by millions of miles on U.S. roads.

Why Is FSD Not Available in Europe?

FSD has been transforming driving in the United States October 2020 — over five years of real-world use, billions of miles driven, and proven safety gains.

In Europe, FSD remains unavailable despite Tesla's active efforts and data consistently showing it is safe and saving lives. Stricter regulations continue to delay approval — leaving EU drivers without access to this transformative and life-saving technology.

Why this matters?

Every year, over 20.000 people die and more than 2 million are injured on EU roads. FSD Supervised — already proven 4.2x safer than human drivers in the U.S. — could dramatically reduce these numbers by preventing crashes before they happen.

Europe is falling behind. While the U.S. has benefited from five years of FSD progress, overly strict regulations here slow or block life-saving innovation. Delaying FSD doesn't just limit convenience — it costs lives, prolongs suffering, and hinders a better quality of life for millions of drivers.
